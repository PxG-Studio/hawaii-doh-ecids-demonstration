### 2. Introduction & Purpose
- Located in the Early Intervention Section (EIS), Children with Special Health Needs Branch (CSHNB), Family Health Services Division (FHSD).
- EIS is responsible for implementing Part C of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), the Hawaii Early Intervention Policies and Procedures, and relevant Hawaii Revised Statutes (HRS) including provision of services for children birth to three with special health needs.
- The Lead Agency Support team develops procedural guidelines to ensure IDEA Part C compliance, evidence-based early intervention, and quality services.
- The Lead Agency Support team reviews and summarizes data for reporting to the US Department of Education (DOE) Office of Special Education Programs (OSEP).
- Responsible for ongoing training and support for early intervention staff and families related to Part C requirements.
- **Primary Purpose:** Assist in the administration, planning, and evaluation of a comprehensive system of personnel development (CSPD) as required by Part C. Assist in responding to early intervention needs, supporting new projects, program monitoring, and collaborating with community-based agencies. A family-centered, community-based, culturally competent, collaborative approach is the basis for all training and professional development.

### 3. Major Duties and Responsibilities

#### A. Consultation and Training (75%)
1. Conduct trainings as assigned for early intervention providers, community partners, and families.
2. Distribute and compile periodic needs assessment surveys from EI providers, community partners, and families to identify training needs and priorities.
3. Log and assign trainings based on assignment protocol, track status, and report on training requests for EI training activities.
4. Disseminate information on training activities to EI providers, community partners, and families.
5. Write training evaluation reports based on evaluations by staff, providers, community representatives, and families.
6. Track ongoing training activities via spreadsheets and/or databases to ensure EI personnel are trained/retrained to meet early intervention standards of evidence-based practices.
7. Coordinate ongoing training and professional development activities with public and private universities, community colleges, agencies, and departments.
8. Coordinate and/or attend EI training and public awareness sessions requested by universities, community groups, or state agencies.
9. Attend EI Training Team meetings as scheduled.

#### B. On-Site Monitoring (20%)
1. Review EI child records (hard copy and database) to ensure programs are compliant with IDEA Part C timelines and requirements (IFSPs, consents, progress notes, evaluations).
2. Review IFSPs using a checklist to ensure all required components, including a transition plan, are included.
3. Compare IFSPs with services provided to ensure services are implemented as written.
4. Participate as a member of the EI Monitoring Team in program monitoring of early intervention state and POS programs to ensure compliance with IDEA Part C, Hawaii Policies, and federal standards (e.g., ADA, OSHA).
5. Prepare monitoring tools by entering data into the Self-Assessment Monitoring (SAM) tool.
6. Enter SAM tool data into the access database upon completion of on-site monitoring.
7. Attend EI Monitoring Team meetings as requested.

#### C. Other Related Duties (5%)
